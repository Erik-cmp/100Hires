## Post 2

- Source: [Google SERPs: Reddit is the #1 most popular domain](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/timsoulo_google-serps-reddit-is-the-1-most-popular-share-7377203388297568256-b1ez/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADeDmuEBWrqFxjlkKuiyfURJ38rOTztV0i4)

> - Google SERPs: Reddit is the #1 most popular domain (by far).
> - Bing SERPs: Reddit is #193.
>
> ^ this comes from Glen Allsopp’s recent (internal) study of 10,000 “best product” queries.
>
> It’s fascinating how Google leans so heavily on Reddit in search results, while Bing almost ignores it.
>
> And honestly, it doesn’t feel “algorithmic,” does it? More like someone at Google just said: “Let’s push Reddit.”
>
> A few other highlights:
>
> ▪️ Forbes was the slight winner on Bing (17th on Google).
> ▪️ Only 2 of the top 10 domains overlapped (NYT & Consumer Reports).
> ▪️ Total ranked domains were similar (Bing: 12,245 vs Google: 12,419).
> ▪️ Bing ranked far more individual URLs (120K vs 82K)
> ▪️ Questionable websites are much more likely to rank well on Bing
>
> ...
>
> I wonder what is stopping Bing from featuring Reddit more in their search results? 🤔
